A little road trip in the Andes Cordillera, Argentina

I have always enjoyed travel types that do not include five-stars hotels and all-day-long sun catching on a beach or alongside a pool. Of course, I do enjoy some chilling time, but it has to be part of a wilder trip.

In April 2016, after months of preparation and money-gathering, I took a plane with a great friend to make a dream come true: taking part fo the third edition of "Trophee Roses des Andes", a wwomen-only roadtrip accross the North of Argentina.

No hotel. No GPS. No comfort.
Just a map, a roadbook and an amazing off-road 4x4 truck...

7 days in the province of Salta

After a 13 hours flight from Paris to Buenos Aires, followed by a 2 hours internal flight to Salta, we met the 118 other women we were going to travel with. More important, we discovered the 60 offroads vehicles which were going to be our home for seven days.

The "Trophee Roses des Andes" is a 2500km (1500 miles) roadtrip in the Province of Salta, one area in the very north of the country. 2500km to drive within 7 days. With no GPS, but only a compass, a map and a roadbook.

What makes this experience so outstanding is the diversity of landscape I discovered in such a few days. From deserts to dried rios, including mountain and almost lunar landscapes, Argentina is a surprising country.

Even if this travel was about going accross deserted roads, I also had the opportunity to meet with few argentinians living in the most isolated villages. Believe me: those people knows how to welcome you!

What this trip was really about

The first time I ever did a roadtrip was in 2008, while going accross Morocco with my parents for almost three weeks. Once I discovered this way of travelling, I wanted to do it again and again.

Unfortunately, it can cost a lot to do roadtrip. More over if you want to discover a country far from yours. Even more if you plan to do so with an offroad vehicle.

Argentina is approximately 11 500 km far from France (7150 miles). To discover this country all the way from Europe, you have to take a 12 hours flight. Not something you do every day. Neither one everybody can afford. I couldn't.

Luckily, some amazing french people (Desertours) made it possible for me and several other women to do so.
This company organize various offroads challenges each year accross the world. They do so because their CEO is an ex-pilote of the Paris-Dakar. He always dreamed about making such experiences available to more people, including students (with the "4L Trophy", one I have also experiment) and women.
